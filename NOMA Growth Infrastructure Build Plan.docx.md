# **1\. Executive Summary**

What NOMA has not yet built is the systems infrastructure to convert this momentum into scalable, repeatable revenue. The Shopify store is live and functional. Klaviyo exists but is empty. There is no influencer pipeline, no automated email/SMS flows, no structured content calendar, and no repeatable drop launch playbook.

This document outlines the full scope of systems that need to be built — organized into five phases, sequenced by dependency and impact. Each phase includes specific deliverables, platform-level implementation details, timelines, and success metrics. The goal is to create a growth infrastructure that compounds: every system feeds the next, and the whole is greater than the sum of its parts.

The drop system wires it all together. Wholesale and PR come last because by then, you have data and proof of performance to bring to those conversations.

# **4\. Phase 1: Revenue Foundation**

KLAVIYO \+ SHOPIFY INTEGRATION — WEEKS 1–3

## **Why This Comes First**

Every other system you build: content, influencers, drops, generates attention. But without an owned channel to capture that attention, it leaks. Klaviyo is the funnel that converts impressions into subscribers and subscribers into revenue. According to Klaviyo’s own benchmark data, automated email flows generate 30x more revenue per recipient than one-off campaigns. For fashion brands specifically, 30% of Klaviyo-attributed revenue typically comes from flows.

Right now, NOMA has 110K+ accounts reached in the last 30 days and zero automated capture. Every day without this system running is money left on the table.

## **4.1 Integration & Technical Setup**

**Shopify-Klaviyo Connection**

* Verify Klaviyo app is installed in Shopify admin

* Sync customer profiles, order history, and product catalog

* Enable onsite behavioral tracking: Viewed Product, Added to Cart, Started Checkout, Placed Order

* Configure branded sending domain for deliverability (e.g., mail.houseofnoma.com)

* Sync Shopify email subscribers to main Klaviyo list

* Set up separate lists for email vs. SMS subscribers

* Enable Extended ID for broader profile identification

**GA4 Integration**

* Audit existing GA4 setup on Shopify

* Ensure e-commerce tracking is active (purchases, add to cart, begin checkout)

* Connect GA4 data to Klaviyo for attribution reporting

## **4.2 List Growth & Capture Points**

The goal is to build the email list from every touchpoint. According to Klaviyo’s 2026 audit findings, list growth is often a user experience problem — brands have demand but make people work too hard to subscribe, especially on mobile.

**Signup Form Strategy**

| FORM TYPE | TRIGGER / PLACEMENT | OFFER / COPY |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Timed Pop-up | 8–10 seconds after page load (mobile: 15 sec) | "Join the Timeline" — first access to drops \+ 10% off first order |
| Exit-Intent Pop-up | Desktop only, cursor moves to close/nav bar | "Before you go" — early drop access, no discount needed |
| Footer Embed | Persistent on all pages | Simple email capture — "Stay in the NOMA timeline" |
| Landing Page | Dedicated URL for social bio links and ads | "Get First Access" — captures email \+ optional SMS consent |
| Checkout Opt-in | Shopify checkout page | Custom label: "Get drop announcements and early access via email" |

## **4.3 Core Automated Flows**

Klaviyo’s benchmark data from nearly 100 account audits in 2025 identified these as the highest-impact flows. Each flow below is specified with trigger, timing, number of emails, and content focus tailored to NOMA’s brand and drop-based model.

**Flow 1: Welcome Series (5–7 Emails Over 30 Days)**

Triggered when someone subscribes to the email list. This is the single highest-revenue automated flow for most brands. Key insight from Klaviyo: split by purchase status. A new subscriber and a new customer need different messages.

**Email 1 (Immediate):** Deliver the welcome offer (10% off). Clean, minimal design. Hero image from Collection 13\. Subject: "Welcome to the Timeline."

**Email 2 (Day 2):** Brand story — Amon’s journey from the Bronx, 11 years of building without investors. This builds trust and emotional connection. Link to "The Making of NOMA" content.

**Email 3 (Day 5):** Product education — hero pieces from Youthful Ignorance. Styling inspiration. Social proof (celebrity cosigns, press mentions). Link to shop.

**Email 4 (Day 10):** Community — "Why NOMA collectors keep coming back." UGC or customer stories. Reinforce the cultural movement angle.

**Email 5 (Day 15):** The NOMA philosophy — deeper dive into nostalgia, minimalism, craft. Position the brand as a long-term relationship, not a one-time purchase.

**Email 6 (Day 22):** Reminder of welcome offer if not yet purchased. Urgency without desperation.

**Email 7 (Day 30):** Non-purchaser path only: final welcome touchpoint. Highlight upcoming drop dates, invite to follow on social.

**Flow 2: Abandoned Cart (3 Emails)**

Triggered when a shopper adds items to cart but doesn’t complete purchase. Klaviyo benchmark: 35–40% open rate, 15–20% conversion rate.

**Email 1 (1–4 hours):** Reminder with dynamic product image. Clean and simple. No discount yet. Subject: "Still thinking it over?"

**Email 2 (24 hours):** Add social proof — press quotes, community photos. Address common objections (fit, quality, shipping).

**Email 3 (48 hours):** Final nudge. For high-value carts ($300+), consider a small incentive (free shipping or exclusive add-on). For standard carts, create urgency through scarcity: "Limited quantities remain."

**Flow 3: Browse Abandonment (2 Emails)**

Triggered when an identified visitor views a product but doesn’t add to cart. Benchmark: 30–35% open rate, 3–5% conversion.

**Email 1 (2–4 hours):** "We noticed you looking at \[Product\]." Dynamic product recommendation block.

**Email 2 (24 hours):** Styling suggestions featuring the viewed product. Cross-sell complementary pieces from the collection.

**Flow 4: Post-Purchase (3–4 Emails)**

Triggered after order confirmation. This is where you build lifetime value and reduce returns. Benchmark: 40–45% open rate, 10–15% repeat purchase rate.

**Email 1 (Immediate):** Order confirmation with brand storytelling. Not just a receipt — welcome them to the NOMA community.

**Email 2 (3 days post-delivery):** Styling guide for their purchase. "How to wear your \[piece\]." Care instructions. Link to community content.

**Email 3 (14 days):** Cross-sell complementary pieces. "Complete the look." Dynamic recommendations based on purchase.

**Email 4 (30 days):** Invitation to share — UGC prompt, affiliate code offer, social tag encouragement.

**Flow 5: Win-Back (3 Emails)**

Triggered when a previous customer hasn’t purchased in 90+ days. Benchmark: 25–30% open rate, 5–8% reactivation.

**Email 1 (90 days):** "It’s been a minute." Highlight what’s new since their last purchase. New collection lookbook.

**Email 2 (120 days):** Exclusive returning customer offer or early access to next drop.

**Email 3 (150 days):** Final touchpoint. Emotional appeal to the brand relationship. "The timeline continues."

**Flow 6: Back-in-Stock Notification**

Triggered when a previously sold-out item is restocked. Critical for the scarcity model — these subscribers already showed purchase intent.

**Single Email (Immediate on restock):** "\[Product\] is back. Limited quantities." Strong urgency. Direct link to purchase. This flow typically has the highest conversion rate of any automated email.

## **4.4 Segmentation Architecture**

Clean segmentation is the foundation of effective email marketing. Build these segments from day one:

| SEGMENT | DEFINITION & USE |
| :---- | :---- |
| Engaged Subscribers | Opened or clicked email in last 90 days. Primary campaign audience. |
| Never Purchased | On list but $0 spent. Welcome flow optimization, first-purchase incentives. |
| One-Time Buyers | Single order. Post-purchase nurture, cross-sell focus. |
| Repeat Customers | 2+ orders. VIP treatment, early drop access, loyalty. |
| VIP / Top 10% | Highest LTV. Exclusive previews, personal outreach. |
| Lapsed (90+ days) | Previously active, gone quiet. Win-back flow target. |
| Drop Hype List | Opted into "first access" for new collections. Drop announcement priority. |
| SMS Subscribers | Opted into text messages. Drop day alerts, flash restocks, event invites. |

## **4.5 Branded Email Templates**

All email templates must reflect NOMA’s visual identity: minimal, editorial, high-contrast. Use Klaviyo’s drag-and-drop editor to build modular templates that can be reused across flows and campaigns.

**Color palette:** Primary background: white/off-white (\#F5F2EE). Accent: NOMA red (\#8B2332). Text: near-black (\#1A1A1A). Secondary text: warm gray.

**Typography:** Georgia or similar serif for headlines. Clean sans-serif for body. Match the website’s editorial feel.

**Imagery:** Full-width hero images. Campaign photography, runway shots, lifestyle editorial. Never stock photos.

**Layout:** Single-column for mobile optimization. Generous whitespace. One clear CTA per email.

# **5\. Phase 2: Content Engine**

SOCIAL CALENDAR & CONTENT SYSTEMS — WEEKS 3–6

## **Why This Comes Second**

With Klaviyo capturing attention, the content engine is what generates that attention consistently. NOMA’s social data shows the brand gets outsized engagement when it posts authentic, story-driven content (12.84% engagement on the “Summer To-Do List / Wear Noma” post vs. 0.33% average). The content system needs to produce more of what works, on a predictable schedule.

## **5.1 Content Pillar Framework**

Every piece of content maps to one of four pillars. This prevents random posting and ensures the feed tells a cohesive brand story:

| PILLAR | CONTENT TYPES | PURPOSE | FREQUENCY |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Product | Editorial photos, styling edits, detail shots, pricing reveals | Drive desire & sales | 1–2x/week |
| Culture | NYC, music, art, Bronx heritage, Ghanaian roots, community events | Build brand world | 1x/week |
| Process | Behind-the-scenes, fabric sourcing, design sketches, factory visits | Build trust & craft cred | 1x/week |
| Community | Customer photos, event recaps, repost/UGC, creator features | Social proof & loyalty | 1x/week |

## **5.2 Weekly Posting Cadence**

Based on NOMA’s social data showing Wednesday as peak reach day, the weekly calendar anchors around a Wednesday hero post:

**Monday:** Process/BTS content (Reel or carousel). Ease into the week with something intimate and craft-focused.

**Wednesday:** Hero content drop — product editorial, new collection reveal, or major campaign piece. This is the anchor.

**Friday:** Culture or community content (Reel). Music crossover, NYC life, customer features. End the week with personality.

**Saturday/Sunday (optional):** Story-only content. Lifestyle reposts, mood boards, archive throwbacks. Low-lift, high-authenticity.

Total: 3–4 feed posts per week, supplemented by 4–5 Stories per week. Quality over quantity — every post should feel like it belongs in a fashion editorial, not a product catalog.

## **5.3 Four Editorial Content Series**

**1\. Art Through Fabric**

**Format:** Short-form video (30–60 seconds), highly stylized

**Platforms:** Instagram Reels, TikTok

**Focus:** Texture close-ups, fabric movement, garment construction details. No talking head — purely visual and cinematic.

**Cadence:** 2x/month

**Goal:** Drive product desire through sensory storytelling

**2\. NOMA.WAV**

**Format:** Music-fashion crossover content (60–90 seconds)

**Platforms:** Instagram Reels, TikTok, YouTube Shorts

**Focus:** Underground artists wearing NOMA in raw, authentic environments. Blend the brand’s aesthetic with diaspora sounds. Think listening party meets lookbook.

**Cadence:** 2x/month

**Goal:** Build cultural relevance and reach new audiences through music crossover

**3\. The Archive Project**

**Format:** Carousel posts, short video essays

**Platforms:** Instagram (primary), editorial blog

**Focus:** Recontextualizing 11 years of NOMA history for new audiences. Past collections, pivotal moments, design evolution, early brand photos. “This is Collection 3, here’s the story behind it.”

**Cadence:** 2x/month

**Goal:** Create depth and legacy narrative. New followers discover the timeline.

**4\. The Making of NOMA**

**Format:** BTS video (1–3 minutes), Stories series

**Platforms:** Instagram, TikTok

**Focus:** From sketches and fabric sourcing to final runway execution. Show the work. Factory visits, fitting sessions, design decisions.

**Cadence:** 2x/month

**Goal:** Build trust through transparency. Let people see that NOMA is a real operation with real craft.

## **5.4 Platform-Specific Guidelines**

**Instagram**

* Reels-first: algorithm prioritizes video. Target 30–90 seconds.

* Carousel posts for Archive Project and product education (5–10 slides)

* Stories: 4–5x/week for behind-the-scenes, polls, countdown timers, swipe-ups to shop

* Instagram Shopping tags on all product-featuring content

* Maintain editorial grid aesthetic — cohesive color palette, no random filler posts

**TikTok**

* Weekly NOMA.WAV content as signature series

* Trend-responsive content but always filtered through NOMA’s aesthetic — never compromise the brand for a trend

* Creator duets and stitch campaigns with seeded influencers

* TikTok Shop integration for shoppable content (target Q2 setup)

* Lower production bar than IG — TikTok rewards rawness. Phone-shot BTS, studio sessions, fabric reveals.

# **6\. Phase 3: Influencer Pipeline & Seeding**

CREATOR STRATEGY — WEEKS 6–9

## **Why This Comes Third**

With Klaviyo capturing leads and a content engine running, influencer seeding amplifies the brand outward. The key shift in 2026 influencer marketing is that smaller creators now drive outsized results. Nano and micro influencers consistently outperform larger accounts in engagement and conversion. A structured seeding program where you send product with no obligation to post and let genuine enthusiasm drive organic content is far more authentic and cost-effective than paid sponsorships at this stage.

Industry data supports this approach: 92% of marketers report that product seeding increases brand awareness, and 76% say it drives sales. For luxury brands specifically, micro influencer engagement rates run between 5–20%, compared to 1–3% for macro influencers.

## **6.1 Tiered Creator Strategy**

| TIER | FOLLOWERS | ROLE | COMPENSATION |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Nano | 1K–10K | Volume \+ authentic UGC. Community-level credibility. | Product gifting only. No content obligation. |
| Micro | 10K–50K | Core seeding targets. Style-aligned, culturally relevant. Content backbone. | Product gifting \+ affiliate code. Top performers get small flat fee. |
| Cultural Validators | 50K+ | Brand credibility. Editorial-quality content. Cross-pollination into new audiences. | Product gifting \+ potential paid collaboration for specific campaigns. |

## **6.2 Creator Selection Criteria**

Not every influencer with the right follower count is right for NOMA. Selection should be based on:

* **Aesthetic Alignment:** Does their feed feel like it could live next to NOMA’s content? Look for clean, editorial, intentional visuals.

* **Cultural Authenticity:** Are they engaged in music, art, or design culture? Do they dress with intention, not trend?

* **Engagement Quality:** Look at comment quality, not just count. Are followers having real conversations or just dropping emojis?

* **Audience Demographics:** Age 22–45, interested in fashion/culture, located in target markets.

* **Content Consistency:** Review the last 60–90 days of posts. Consistent voice, quality, and posting frequency.

* **Brand Safety:** No controversial content, no history of paid posts that feel transactional or off-brand.

This is where the international expansion strategy comes to life — through creators, not storefronts:

| MARKET | TARGET \# | CREATOR PROFILE | WHY |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |

| London | 5–10 | Fashion-forward, editorial-minded, multicultural | Strong appetite for NYC streetwear-luxury aesthetic |
| Paris | 3–5 | High fashion, editorial credibility, cultural curators | Luxury fashion capital, editorial validation |
| Tokyo | 3–5 | Harajuku/streetwear overlap, minimalism-focused | Deep appreciation for American streetwear, design culture |

## **6.4 Seeding Operations**

**Outreach Process**

1. Identify and vet creator (aesthetic, engagement, audience check)

2. Add to influencer database with notes and status

3. Send personalized outreach DM or email (template provided, customized per creator)

4. If interested: collect shipping info, size, and product preferences

5. Ship product with branded packaging and personal note from the brand

6. Include content brief (optional — not required, just suggestions)

7. Follow up 5–7 days after delivery with a check-in

8. Track whether they post, log content, measure engagement

9. Top performers: offer affiliate code, deepen relationship

**Content Brief for Seeded Creators**

This is a suggestion guide, not a mandate. The whole point of seeding is authenticity. The brief should include:

* Brand background: one-paragraph summary of NOMA’s story and values

* Collection context: what the piece is, what collection it’s from, the story behind it

* Visual references: 3–5 images showing NOMA’s aesthetic

* Hashtags: \#NOMA \#HouseOfNOMA \#YouthfulIgnorance \#NOMAtimeline

* Tag: @houseofnoma

* Suggested formats: “Style it your way, show us how you’d wear it”

* Explicitly state: no obligation to post. If you love it, share it. If not, enjoy the piece.

## **6.5 Tracking & Measurement**

| METRIC | HOW TO TRACK |
| :---- | :---- |

| UGC Volume | \# of content pieces generated per quarter from seeding |

# **7\. Phase 4: Drop Launch System**

THE REPEATABLE PLAYBOOK — WEEKS 9–12

## **Why This Comes Fourth**

This is where everything connects. The Klaviyo flows, the content engine, the influencer network — they all converge around the drop. NOMA’s business model is built on engineered scarcity with a target of 90%+ sell-through within 48 hours. The drop launch system is the operational playbook that makes that target achievable and repeatable.

## **7.1 Drop Launch Timeline**

**T-14 Days: Hype Phase**

* Teaser content begins on social (cryptic imagery, fabric close-ups, date hints)

* Klaviyo: Send “something’s coming” email to Drop Hype List segment

* Influencer seeding: ship hero pieces to 10–15 aligned creators

* Archive Project post: connect new drop to NOMA’s timeline

**T-7 Days: Reveal Phase**

* Full collection reveal on social (product imagery, key looks, pricing)

* Klaviyo: Collection preview email with product photography to full engaged list

* SMS: First access announcement to SMS subscribers

* IG Stories: Countdown sticker activations

* TikTok: Behind-the-scenes of the collection, styling previews

* Influencer content starts going live from seeded creators

**T-1 Day: Final Push**

* Klaviyo: “Tomorrow” reminder email with direct links to products

* SMS: Time-specific drop alert (“Drops at 12pm EST tomorrow”)

* Social: Final countdown Stories, last teaser Reel

* Email and social both link to the same landing page / collection page

**Drop Day (T-0)**

* Products go live on Shopify at designated time

* Klaviyo: Drop is live email (immediate, to full engaged list \+ hype list)

* SMS: Drop is live text (to SMS subscribers)

* Social: Post announcing drop, Stories with swipe-up links, IG Shopping tags

* Monitor Shopify analytics in real-time: traffic, add-to-carts, conversions

* Abandoned cart flow kicks in automatically for drop-day browsers

**T+1 to T+3: Post-Drop**

* Social: Sold-out callouts, restock interest capture, community celebration

* Klaviyo: Thank you email to purchasers (post-purchase flow begins)

* Klaviyo: Back-in-stock signup for sold-out items

* Influencer content continues posting (stagger for sustained visibility)

* Performance review: sell-through %, revenue, email/SMS contribution, top products

## **7.2 Drop Day Execution Checklist**

* All products published and live on Shopify

* Collection page updated with hero imagery

* Klaviyo drop email scheduled and verified

* SMS blast queued

* Social posts scheduled across IG and TikTok

* IG Shopping tags active on all product posts

* Abandoned cart flow active and tested

* Back-in-stock capture enabled for all SKUs

* Team on standby for customer service (sizing questions, shipping inquiries)

* Real-time dashboard open: Shopify analytics \+ Klaviyo reporting

## **7.3 Phase 4 Deliverables Checklist**

## **8.3 International Creator Strategy**

The international expansion is not about opening stores or wholesale accounts abroad — it’s about sending product to the right creators in cultural capitals and letting them generate organic content and buzz. Ship a biker jacket to London, get an editorial shoot back. Send pieces to Lagos, get authentic diaspora-connected content. This is expansion through cultural presence, not retail footprint.

**Execution**

* Identify 3–5 creators per international market (from Phase 3 database)

* Create market-specific content briefs that contextualize NOMA within local culture

* Ship hero pieces (biker jackets, outerwear — high-impact, photographable items)

* Coordinate timing: stagger international content to maintain sustained global visibility

* Repurpose international creator content for NOMA’s own social channels (with permission/licensing)

* Track which markets generate the most engagement — this data informs future wholesale and pop-up decisions
